most saas will die

2026-04-02

most saas isn't sophisticated technology. it's business logic someone wrote once, wrapped in a ui, and charged you $100/month for forever.

insurance quoting. invoice reconciliation. compliance checklists. crm workflows. none of this is hard. it's just tedious enough that someone built a product 10-15 years ago around it and slapped an annual contract on top.

companies and individuals are starting to realise they can open agentic tooling built on top of frontier models, describe what they need in a paragraph, and have a working version of what they were paying five figures a year for, all within minutes. not a prototype. but a working tool.

and this only accelerates. scaling laws aren't slowing down; we're still on the curve. more compute, better architectures, longer context, stronger reasoning. every capability gain compounds into a direct reduction in the cost of producing software. the tooling layer is catching up too: agentic coding environments are turning natural language into deployed applications with increasingly minimal human oversight.

the saas model was built on a simple arbitrage, custom software was too expensive to build and maintain, so renting someone else's was always the rational choice. that arbitrage is collapsing. when the marginal cost of producing software falls below the marginal cost of renting it, there is no longer a rational choice. there's just the obvious one.
